# Flex And Catch-Up Policy

This plan is designed for an employed learner with limited and variable study time. The
default weekly budget is five core sessions of up to two hours, one catch-up session, and
one rest or optional stretch session.

## Weekly Rhythm

- Five core sessions cover the planned LLM, GPU, bridge, behavioral, and assessment work.
- One catch-up session absorbs missed work, review, or a lighter practice drill.
- One rest or optional stretch session protects recovery and avoids turning missed work
  into a permanent backlog.

## Minimum Viable Completion

Each week counts as complete enough to move forward if these items are done:

- One LLM topic.
- One GPU topic.
- One bridge exercise.
- One assessment or reflection.

Behavioral practice should still happen when possible, but it can be folded into the
catch-up session during a demanding work week.

## Missed-Day Recovery

If one day is missed, move that session into the catch-up slot. Keep the rest of the week
unchanged and avoid doubling the next session unless energy is high.

If two days are missed, protect the minimum viable completion list first. Defer stretch
reading, extra notes, or optional drills to the next catch-up window.

If an entire week is missed, do a recovery week instead of trying to complete two full
weeks at once. Finish the minimum viable items, write a short weak-area note, and resume
the next planned week.

The goal is durable interview readiness, not perfect adherence to a calendar.
